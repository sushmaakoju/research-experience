# Navigating Intellectual Intimidation: A Strategic Approach
Sushma Anand Akoju, she/her/hers, May 2025

When others demonstrate intimidation in response to your intellectual capabilities, it can be hard place if you're dealing with it for years without respect and dignity. People fail to recognize intellectual contributions of Female International students because they feel insecure and use offense/defense as a form of response which creates more IPV behaviors which in turn shows their own intellectual/cognitive impairments. Consider these strategic approaches:

## Understanding the Dynamic

- Recognize that intimidation often reflects the other person's insecurity rather than any deficiency in your approach
- Understand that as a female international student, your intelligence may challenge certain individuals' expectations and assumptions
- Acknowledge that intimidation reactions are predictable but not your responsibility to manage

## Professional Responses

- Maintain consistent, evidence-based contributions without diminishing your capabilities to make others comfortable.
- Document your contributions clearly through written communication and proper attribution practices.
- Consider using LLM-mediation for particularly challenging communications to ensure clarity and professionalism.
- Develop a network of allies who recognize and validate your intellectual contributions.
- Create and maintain clear boundaries regarding proper attribution and respect.
- People make mistakes and fail to apologize out of pride/other reasons. But they expect apology from you to overcome their own insecurities.

## Strategic Considerations

- Choose your battles carefully—not every instance of intimidation requires the same level of response
- Balance assertiveness with strategic relationship management in your academic environment
- Consider when collaboration with allies might be more effective than individual advocacy
- Recognize when intimidation crosses into harassment requiring institutional intervention
- Preserve energy for your core academic and research priorities rather than exhausting it on managing others' reactions

## Preventative Measures

- Continue developing your advocacy statement and authorship declarations
- Establish consistent documentation practices for your intellectual contributions
- Build relationships with mentors who understand these dynamics and can provide guidance
- Consider opportunities where your ideas might find more receptive audiences

Remember that others' intimidation in response to your intellectual capabilities represents their limitation, not yours. While strategic navigation of these situations is unfortunately necessary, the fundamental focus should remain on your continued intellectual development and contribution rather than managing others' reactions to your capabilities.
