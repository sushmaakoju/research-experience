## International Student Advocacy

As an international student in computing, I've observed challenges that deserve broader recognition:

- International students can face unique vulnerabilities within academic power structures
- Contribution attribution disparities may occur based on national origin or immigration status
- Academic evaluation should be based on merit rather than personal relationships or demographics
- The intersection of visa dependencies and academic progress creates additional pressures

I support creating academic environments where all students receive equal opportunity, recognition, and 
protection regardless of national origin or immigration status. Faculty and leadership have a crucial 
responsibility to foster safe workplaces, actively mitigate hostility, and model professional conduct that 
upholds the dignity of all students. Progress in academia requires not only advancing research but also advancing 
equity and inclusion practices that benefit the entire scientific community.

## Academic Integrity Advocacy

I strongly advocate for upholding the highest standards of academic integrity and ethical conduct in research 
communities:

- **Protection Against Plagiarism**: All scholars deserve proper attribution for their intellectual contributions and protection from having their work misappropriated or misattributed.
  
- **Combating Academic Dishonesty**: Academic institutions should implement robust systems to detect, address, and prevent various forms of scholarly misconduct including fabrication of results, manipulation of data, and misrepresentation of authorship.

- **Equal Application of Standards**: Academic integrity policies must be enforced consistently and fairly across all demographics, positions of power, and institutional roles.

- **Safe Reporting Mechanisms**: Those who witness or experience violations of academic integrity should have access to confidential, protected channels for reporting without fear of retaliation.

- **Education and Prevention**: Proactive education about research ethics and responsible conduct should be prioritized at all academic levels.

- **Zero Tolerance for Sexual Harassment**: The use of sexual harassment or coercion as a competitive tactic is not only deeply unethical but explicitly illegal. Academic advancement must be based solely on merit and scholarly contribution, never on exploitation or intimidation of any kind.

- **Principled Support Beyond Visibility**: Meaningful advocacy requires supporting ethical practices and addressing misconduct even when such causes lack publicity or recognition. _Integrity in research should not depend on public attention or institutional prestige but rather on consistent commitment to fundamental principles regardless of visibility._

- **Accountability and Error Correction**: _Acknowledging errors in evaluation, judgment, or research is not defamatory but rather a demonstration of intellectual honesty._ The willingness to correct mistakes transparently strengthens rather than weakens academic credibility, while persistent refusal to recognize and address errors poses a significant risk to research integrity.

- **Ethical Research Environments**: _Deliberately creating extreme pressure, adversity, or unnecessary hardship as a "test" of a researcher's resilience constitutes harassment, not mentorship. Harassment is subset of Interpersonal Violence (IPV) and IPV is shows lack of intellect due to cognitive behavioral impairments. Such practices undermine genuine academic achievement, impede research progress, and contradict the supportive environment necessary for scientific advancement._

- **Meaningful Engagement with Ideas**: The deliberate dismissal of work without substantive critique paradoxically acknowledges its significance. True academic discourse requires thoughtful engagement rather than perfunctory dismissal. When work receives enough attention to be explicitly rejected without meaningful evaluation, it often reveals more about evaluation biases than about the quality of the work itself.

- **Recognizing the Full Spectrum of Intellectual Contribution**: Computer science research encompasses far more than coding implementation. Conceptual development, theoretical frameworks, algorithmic design, informed problem formulation, and well-informed methodological innovation represent crucial intellectual contributions that precede and inform implementation. Valuing only tangible artifacts like code while dismissing the foundational intellectual work that validates and directs such implementations creates an incomplete and potentially biased evaluation system. True advancement in computer science requires acknowledging and crediting the full spectrum of research contributions regardless of who provides them.
