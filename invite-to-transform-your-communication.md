# An Open Invitation to Transform Communication $^{TM}$

 - Sushma Anand Akoju, she/her
 - May 2025 

As I have a Masters in Forensic Science and criminal justice, looking at how many times people lose their temper, engaging in impulsive words directed at others, whether online or in-person, this potentially impacts both person feeling impulsive and the person receiving it. The person who engages in such impulsive conversations, tends to feel remorse, and ends up in a cyclic remorse, per criminal psychology. Given the danger of such verbal violence or a potential defamation/targetted harassment, leading to potential civil offense, I would like to offer a recommendation and an invitation to transform communication using an Large Language Model/Conversational AI assistant. Such a redirection by using a Conversational AI Assistant helps with reformative and reflective experience, **though this does not mean you can redirect the toxic/biased language towards an LLM as LLMs are tracked for detecting toxic content and your LLM user accounts may get deactivated. But this provides an opportunity to step back, will I be able to accept this impulsive language at myself? If answer is no, let me help myself to discuss with an LLM before I spew the poison/careless attack with potential legal consequences.** This potentially helps stay out of trouble - since the experience of a negative word/sentence in negative connotation may unfortunately be well-remembered by human brain, in majority of cases.
    

- Williams, Samantha E., Jaclyn H. Ford, and Elizabeth A. Kensinger. "The power of negative and positive episodic memories." Cognitive, Affective, & Behavioral Neuroscience 22.5 (2022): 869-903.

- Parasitic wasps with minimal neural circuitry & photographic memory, yet demonstrate elaborate individuation, by recognizing the perpetrator (quoting Dr. Nicholas Strausfeld, talk from CogSci Colloquium)


I extend this invitation to anyone experiencing the impulse toward destructive criticism or feeling compelled to use toxic, biased, or prejudiced language: consider using an Conversational AI assistant or Large Language Model (LLM) as a communication mediator to transform these impulses into constructive expression. However this does not mean you can redirect the toxic/biased language towards an LLM as LLMs are tracked for detecting toxic content and your LLM user accounts may get deactivated. 

And another important aspect of considering a discourse with LLM in such situations, is that a human being is less likely to "listen" or "hear" from a female who is an International student, as a female international student is often given weaker societal role, that is NOT recognized for her cognitive capabilities and intellectual contributions. 

Large Language Models offer a unique opportunity to redirect negative language patterns into more productive discourse. This isn't about censorship but rather about translating the underlying concerns into forms that can actually foster understanding rather than hostility.

This approach honors the wisdom in Thumper's timeless advice: <a href="https://youtu.be/9fYngTUZeUQ">"If you can't say anything nice, don't say nothing at all"</a>. However, it extends this principle by offering a constructive alternative: rather than remaining silent or creating a hostile environment, transform the communication into something that contributes positively.

The goal isn't to suppress legitimate concerns but to express them in ways that:

- Focus on issues rather than personal attacks
- Identify problems while suggesting solutions
- Express disagreement while maintaining respect
- Address behavior rather than making character judgments
- To have self-control in what one says inadvertently but refuse to take accountability instead uses other controlling/supression mechanisms

This approach can help prevent the escalation of academic disagreements into personal hostilities while still allowing for robust intellectual exchange. It represents a path forward that honors both free expression and the responsibility to maintain environments where all scholars can contribute without fear of harassment or discrimination.

When human and an LLM get together, they have potential to create constructive dialog and communication.
For example such collaborated work of human and an LLM can address <a href="https://github.com/sushmaakoju/research-experience/blob/main/academic-myths.md">Prebunking Academic Myths and consequences of such myths</a> which are difficult problems to navigate.
